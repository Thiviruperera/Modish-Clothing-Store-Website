Hypebeast Culture

A Deeper Dwelve



Emphasis on Name brands

What is a hypebeast, exactly?




With a growing trend of prominent brand names and logos on clothing, there has been a development of "hypebeast culture" connected to streetwear as of the mid-2000s. This trend is inspired by a 1990s fashion for clothing covered in brand names and logos . A lot of hypebeast fashion is white or black with pops of bright colors like red, teal, or orange.

What kind of style?



Hypebeasts usually wear a variety of name brands at once to boast their affluence and display popular trends. Growth of the Hypebeast culture has led to the consumption of many name brands, some including, Bape, Supreme, Off White, Palace, Louis Vuitton, Balenciaga, and Gucci. Hypebeast culture has led to the growth of buying and reselling these name brand items with prices varying on the demand for said items.






Supreme Hoodie, price: $4000

Rare Air Jordans, price: $7000

The rarer the better




With a cult like following online, "Hypebeasts" are always on the lookout for more expensive, unique and rare items that are of one of a kind making them stand apart from the rest. This constant hunger for more unique, rare and expensive items keeps the hypebeast style ever changing and it continues to evovlve faster than any style on the market as of 2017. Whether you like this style or not, there's no denying the impact it has had on todays fashion market and youth.
